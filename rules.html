2020 Rules

Due to the current health crisis, Houston Underground is pleased to offer a fully-online Solo contest

Last updated March 31, 2020.

General

Contestants must be currently enrolled in a high school or junior high school/middle school. If a contestant is not located in the United States, the contestant must be no more than 18 years of age.

Former winners of Houston Underground live competitions are eligible to compete.

Contestants must have taken at least one person-to-person online lesson of some sort (Skype, Facetime, Zoom, Microsoft Teams, etc.) between March 1, 2020 and April 15, 2020. Lessons may be provided by any private lesson instructor, and do not necessarily need to be from anyone connected to Houston Underground.

Applications are $40 USD per student, payable via PayPal.

A minimum of 15 applications must be received to hold the competition. If less than 15 applications are received, all applicants will receive their money back.

Applications

Applications are available on the Houston Underground website.

Recordings must contain audio AND video, and video must show all performers at all times.

Applications must consist of two recordings:

  • Any etude from Ferling 48 Famous Studies
  • Any additional repertoire

Application recordings must be a single take of any performance, without any cuts or overdubs.

Application videos may be taken from pre-quarantine situations, including solo and ensemble performances, audition recordings, and other previously made recordings, provided they meet all guidelines as stated here.

Videos must be uploaded to YouTube, Vimeo, Google Drive, or any other file-sharing or video-streaming platform. Video URL links must be provided in the application process.

For repertoire with piano parts (i.e., sonatas, concertos, other solos with piano), piano accompaniment in the recording is not required. Pre-recorded accompaniment such as play-along tracks, SmartMusic, Music Minus One, etc., are acceptable. Unaccompanied playing – while not ideal for typical performance settings – is acceptable for this contest due to the current health situation.

Performances without piano parts may skip large periods of rests or silence.

While recording quality is NOT a part of the judges’ rubric, contestants are strongly encouraged to experiment with recording devices to create the best quality audio and video product.

The deadline for applications is April 15th, 2020, at 11:59 PM Central Time Zone.

Prizes

Prizes will begin in the following amounts:

  • First place: $250
  • Second place: $150
  • Third place: $100

Prizes will be made payable via PayPal or personal check.

As applications increase in number, prize money and number of prizes will also increase.

Winners will be announced via email and through the Houston Underground Website on Friday, May 1st, 2020, at 6:00 PM Central Time.
